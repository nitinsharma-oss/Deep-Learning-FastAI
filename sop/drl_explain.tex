%% ==================================================================
The SNR relation has been revised twice at the user's direction:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.35}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Revision} & \textbf{SNR relation} & \textbf{Status}\\
\midrule
Rev.~1 (original) & $v = b^{2}\,P_t\,\gamma\,P_o$ (shared $b$, multiplies) & superseded\\
Rev.~2 & $v_B = \dfrac{\gamma_B P_o}{b_1^{2}}\,P_t$,\quad $v_E = \dfrac{\gamma_E P_o}{b_2^{2}}\,P_t$ & superseded\\
\rowcolor{blue!8}
Rev.~3 (current) & $v_B = \dfrac{\gamma_B\,P_o}{b_1^{2}\,P_t}$,\quad $v_E = \dfrac{\gamma_E\,P_o}{b_2^{2}\,P_t}$ & implemented \& verified\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
In Rev.~3 the transmit power $P_t$ sits in the \emph{denominator} of both SNRs. In the code the
optical constants are folded per link into
\begin{equation}
c_B := \frac{P_o}{b_1^{2}}, \qquad c_E := \frac{P_o}{b_2^{2}},
\qquad\text{so}\qquad
v_B = \frac{c_B\,\gamma_B}{P_t},\qquad v_E = \frac{c_E\,\gamma_E}{P_t}.
\label{eq:snr}
\end{equation}
All SNR-dependent quantities were updated: the environment reward, the closed-form reward
gradient, the water-filling baseline, and CAPG's analytical gradient. The learning
\emph{algorithms} (DDPG, SAC, PPO, ECDPO) are untouched --- they see the channel only through the
reward.

\section{The MDP Formulation Under Revision 3}
%% ==================================================================
\textbf{State.} $s_t = (\gamma_B^{(t)}, \gamma_E^{(t)})$, normalised to $[0,1]^2$, evolving as
AR(1):
\begin{equation}
\gamma^{(t+1)} = \rho\,\gamma^{(t)} + (1-\rho)\,w_t,\qquad
w_t\sim\mathrm{Unif}(0.2,\,3.0),\qquad \rho = 0.85 .
\end{equation}

\textbf{Action.} $a_t = P_t\in[P_{\min},P_{\max}] = [0.1,\,5.0]$, via a sigmoid actor output
affinely mapped to the range.

\textbf{Reward.} With the Rev.~3 SNRs \eqref{eq:snr}, rate law $\log_2(1+Av)$, and the Lagrangian
power penalty ($\bar P = 2.0$, $\lambda = 0.3$):
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
r_t \;=\; \Big[\log_2\!\Big(1 + \frac{A_B\,c_B\,\gamma_B}{P_t}\Big)
- \log_2\!\Big(1 + \frac{A_E\,c_E\,\gamma_E}{P_t}\Big)\Big]^{+}
\;-\;\lambda\,(P_t - \bar P)
\;}
\label{eq:reward}
\end{equation}
with $A_B = 1.5 > A_E = 0.5$.

\textbf{Closed-form reward gradient.} Using
$\frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dP}\log_2(1+t/P) = -\frac{1}{\ln 2}\cdot\frac{t}{P^2+tP}$,
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial r}{\partial P}
= \frac{1}{\ln 2}\left[\frac{-t_B}{P^{2}+t_B P} + \frac{t_E}{P^{2}+t_E P}\right] - \lambda,
\qquad t_B = A_B c_B \gamma_B,\;\; t_E = A_E c_E \gamma_E .
\label{eq:drdp}
\end{equation}
\emph{Verification:} \eqref{eq:drdp} was checked against central-difference numerical
differentiation of \eqref{eq:reward} at $P\in\{0.3, 1.0, 2.5, 4.5\}$ with deliberately asymmetric
constants ($b_1{=}1.3$, $b_2{=}0.9$, $P_o{=}1.2$): agreement to $10^{-9}$--$10^{-10}$.

\section{Critical Finding: Revision 3 Makes the Control Problem Degenerate}
%% ==================================================================
\label{sec:degeneracy}
This section is the most important addition of Revision~3 and must be read before interpreting
any learning curve produced under it.

\subsection{Proof That the Reward Is Monotonically Decreasing in \texorpdfstring{$P_t$}{Pt}}
Positive secrecy requires $t_B > t_E$ (otherwise the $[\cdot]^+$ clips to zero). The secrecy term is
\begin{equation}
S(P) = \log_2\frac{1 + t_B/P}{1 + t_E/P},
\qquad
\frac{\mathrm dS}{\mathrm dP}
= \frac{1}{P\ln 2}\left[\frac{t_E}{P + t_E} - \frac{t_B}{P + t_B}\right].
\end{equation}
Since $x\mapsto \frac{x}{P+x}$ is strictly increasing, $t_B > t_E$ implies
$\frac{t_B}{P+t_B} > \frac{t_E}{P+t_E}$, hence $\mathrm dS/\mathrm dP < 0$: \emph{whenever secrecy
is positive, it strictly decreases with transmit power.} (Intuitively: raising $P_t$ lowers
\emph{both} SNRs in \eqref{eq:snr}, and Bob --- having the larger SNR --- loses more log-capacity
than Eve.) The power penalty $-\lambda(P-\bar P)$ is also strictly decreasing in $P$. Therefore
the total reward \eqref{eq:reward} is strictly decreasing in $P$ \emph{for every channel state},
and the optimal policy is trivially
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\pi^\ast(s) \equiv P_{\min}\quad \text{for all } s.\;}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Empirical Confirmation (Not Just Assertion)}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item Monotonicity was checked numerically on \textbf{500 random channel states} over a 200-point
power grid: strictly decreasing in \emph{all} cases, zero exceptions.
\item The water-filling routine returns $P_{\min}$ at every tested state (it degenerates to a
constant).
\item Trained agents converge to the constant: DDPG and CAPG both reached mean deployed power
$= 0.100 = P_{\min}$ exactly; their eval rewards ($1.98$--$2.00$) approach the constant-$P_{\min}$
baseline ($2.048$).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{What This Means Practically}
Under Revision~3 as literally written, \textbf{there is nothing for DRL to learn}: five
sophisticated algorithms are discovering the number $0.1$. Any learning-curve comparison produced
under this formula measures only how quickly each algorithm finds a constant --- it says nothing
about adaptive power control. This typically indicates one of three issues in the source model,
which should be checked against the reference paper:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item The $P_t$ in the denominator of the source's SNR expression is actually the \emph{noise}
or interference power (very common: $v = \gamma P_{\mathrm{tx}} / (b^2\sigma^2)$), with transmit
power entering the numerator or being absorbed into $P_o$.
\item The source objective contains an additional term that \emph{rewards} high power (throughput
of a second link, harvested energy, a minimum-rate constraint on Bob), creating the missing
trade-off.
\item The adaptation variable in the source is not $P_t$ at all (e.g.\ it is an IRS phase/orientation
variable), and power is fixed.
\end{enumerate}
The code is kept fully general (the bisection water-filling and all agents work for any monotonic
or non-monotonic reward), so correcting the relation once more is a one-line change in
\texttt{wiretap\_env.py}.

\section{Theory Behind Each of the Five Agents}
%% ==================================================================

\subsection{DDPG --- Deterministic Policy Gradient}
Actor $\mu_\theta(s)\!\to\!P$, critic $Q_\phi$. Critic: TD regression to
$y = r + \gamma\,Q_{\phi'}(s',\mu_{\theta'}(s'))$ with soft target updates. Actor:
$\nabla_\theta J = \E_s[\nabla_a Q_\phi(s,a)|_{a=\mu_\theta(s)}\nabla_\theta\mu_\theta(s)]$.
Gaussian exploration noise with linear decay (150k configuration).

\subsection{SAC --- Maximum-Entropy RL}
Squashed-Gaussian actor; objective $\E[\sum_t r_t + \alpha\mathcal H(\pi(\cdot|s_t))]$; twin
critics with min-target and entropy-regularised backup; temperature $\alpha$ auto-tuned toward
target entropy $-1$.

\subsection{PPO --- Clipped Trust Region (On-Policy)}
GAE advantages ($\lambda_{\mathrm{GAE}}{=}0.95$); clipped surrogate
$\E[\min(\rho_t\hat A_t, \mathrm{clip}(\rho_t,1{\pm}\epsilon)\hat A_t)]$, $\epsilon{=}0.2$;
$K{=}8$ epochs per 256-step rollout.

\subsection{ECDPO --- The Hybrid}
Deterministic actor (DDPG) $+$ twin-Q min-target (SAC/TD3) $+$ Bernoulli-entropy bonus
($c_{\mathrm{ent}}{=}0.05$) on the $(0,1)$ action to avoid boundary saturation during learning.

\subsection{CAPG --- Concavity-Aware Policy Gradient (Novel)}
Blends the learned critic gradient with the \emph{exact} analytical gradient \eqref{eq:drdp}:
\begin{equation}
\nabla_\theta J = (1-\beta)\,\nabla_\theta Q_\phi\big(s,\mu_\theta(s)\big)
+ \beta\,\frac{\partial r}{\partial P}\Big|_{P=\mu_\theta(s)}\nabla_\theta\mu_\theta(s),
\qquad \beta = \sigma(\log\beta)\ \text{learnable}.
\end{equation}
Under Rev.~3, $\beta$ rose to $0.86$ within $1{,}500$ steps and CAPG marched directly to
$P_{\min}$ --- the analytical gradient \eqref{eq:drdp} is negative everywhere (Section~\ref{sec:degeneracy}),
so CAPG ``knows'' the answer immediately. This is the degenerate-case behaviour of a mechanism
that, in a non-degenerate problem, is what makes CAPG the fastest and best-performing agent.

\section{Why Learning Curves Fluctuate (Applies to Any Non-Degenerate Revision)}
%% ==================================================================
Four noise sources; the dominant one is measurement:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item \textbf{Monte-Carlo evaluation.} Each point averages $\sim$1000 reward samples of a random
fading process with correlation $\rho=0.85$, so the effective sample count is
$N_{\mathrm{eff}}\approx N\frac{1-\rho}{1+\rho}\approx 81$, giving a standard error of
$\pm 0.01$--$0.02$ per point --- the observed wiggle. Even the deterministic optimal policy shows
the same wiggle under fresh evaluation seeds.
\item \textbf{Policy drift between checkpoints.} Updates occur every step; near the optimum the
reward is flat in $P$, so the induced reward variation stays small.
\item \textbf{Bootstrapped moving targets} (the ``deadly triad''): oscillation around the fixed
point is a property of TD learning, not a bug.
\item \textbf{Minibatch gradient noise}; SAC adds entropy-driven action noise on top, hence its
noisiest early curve.
\end{enumerate}
And the reward can never be a flat line on a fading channel: what converges is the
\emph{expectation} $\rho^\ast$, while any finite-window average inherits the channel's
randomness. Correct convergence criterion: \textbf{oscillation within a fixed band around the
optimal baseline}.

\section{Summary and Recommendation}
%% ==================================================================
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item Revision~3 is \textbf{implemented exactly as specified and verified} (gradient to
$10^{-9}$; agents converge to the provable optimum).
\item But Revision~3 is \textbf{degenerate}: reward strictly decreasing in $P_t$ for every state
$\Rightarrow$ optimal policy $\equiv P_{\min}$ $\Rightarrow$ no adaptive control to learn.
\item \textbf{Action item:} verify against the source paper whether the denominator $P_t$ is in
fact noise power, whether the objective has a power-rewarding term, or whether the true control
variable is something other than $P_t$. Correcting is a one-line change in
\texttt{wiretap\_env.py}; every agent and this guide's analysis then apply unchanged.
\item In any \emph{non-degenerate} version of the problem, the expected ranking remains
CAPG $>$ ECDPO $\approx$ DDPG $>$ PPO $>$ SAC (at moderate horizons), for the structural reasons
in Section~4.
\end{itemize}
