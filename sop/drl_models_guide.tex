\begin{abstract}
This guide documents five deep reinforcement learning (DRL) agents---DDPG, SAC,
PPO, CAPG, and ECDPO---implemented to optimize the common yaw~$\gamma$ and
roll~$\omega$ of an optical intelligent reflecting surface (OIRS) serving $K$
users over a blocked-LoS visible light communication (VLC) link. Unlike a
deterministic point-mirror formulation, the training environment reproduces the
paper's stochastic channel: the incidence cosine $\xi=\cos\psi$ of every
LED--element--user path is drawn from the measurement-based truncated-Laplace
model, so the agents maximize the \emph{expected} achievable data rate (ADR)
under random user equipment (UE) orientation. Every algorithm is trained under
an identical state, action space, reward, network width, and interaction
budget. A common-random-number grid search provides the optimality reference,
and the learned orientations are additionally evaluated on the cross-layer
HARQ-IR Age of Information (AoI) metrics of the analytical framework.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem--Environment Mapping}

\subsection{Why a deterministic environment is misaligned}
A deterministic specular-mirror model maps $(\gamma,\omega)$ to a single
channel gain, admits a closed-form ``bisector'' optimum, and reduces DRL to a
noiseless two-variable search. The analytical framework instead models the UE
orientation as random: the equivalent NLoS gain is the aggregate
\begin{equation}
Z_k=\sum_{l=1}^{M}\sum_{i=1}^{N}X_{(k,i,l)},\qquad
X_{(k,i,l)}=a_k\cos\bigl(\psi_k^{\,i}\bigr),
\label{eq:Zk}
\end{equation}
with $\xi=\cos\psi$ following a truncated-Laplace law on
$[\xi_{\min},\xi_{\max}]$. Consequently (i) the reward observed by the agent is
a random variable, (ii) the optimization target is the \emph{expectation} over
UE orientation, and (iii) with $K>1$ users sharing one $(\gamma,\omega)$, no
closed-form optimum exists. The environment described next restores this
alignment.

\subsection{Stochastic environment (\texttt{oirs\_vlc\_env.py})}
\paragraph{Channel statistics.} For each user $k$, the incidence cosine is
sampled by accept--reject (identical to the MATLAB verification script) from
\begin{equation}
f_\xi(\xi)=\frac{1}{\Lambda}
\exp\!\left(-\frac{|\xi-\mu_{\xi,k}|}{b_\xi}\right),
\qquad \xi\in[\xi_{\min},\xi_{\max}],
\end{equation}
with $b_\xi=0.036$, $\xi_{\min}=0$, $\xi_{\max}=0.9574$ (worked-example
values); $\mu_{\xi,k}$ follows from the mean UE normal and the IRS$\to$user
arrival direction. The exact moments $\E[u]$ and $\E[u^2]$ of the shifted
variable are evaluated with the closed forms of the analytical framework
(Eqs.~6--8 of the paper), and the per-user Gamma shape
$k_{g_k}=MN\,\E[\xi]^2/\mathrm{Var}[\xi]$ and scale
$\theta_{g_k}=a_k\mathrm{Var}[\xi]/\E[\xi]$ (paper Eq.~13) drive the
cross-layer HARQ-IR outage and AoI evaluation.

\paragraph{Orientation-dependent gain.} The common yaw/roll enter through the
unified-reflector steering: with mirror normal
$\mathbf n(\gamma,\omega)=R_y(\omega)R_z(\gamma)(1,0,0)^{\mathsf T}$ and
specular direction $\mathbf d_{\mathrm{ref}}$, the per-user aggregated gain is
\begin{equation}
a_k(\gamma,\omega)=\rho\,\frac{(m+1)A_{\mathrm{pd}}}{2\pi (d_1+d_{2,k})^2}
\cos^m\!\phi\;
\bigl(\mathbf d_{\mathrm{ref}}^{\mathsf T}\mathbf u_k\bigr)_+^{\,\kappa},
\end{equation}
where $\kappa$ is the reflector beam-concentration exponent ($\kappa=20$ for
the throughput study, $\kappa=6$ for the wide-beam fairness study).

\paragraph{MDP.} State: fixed normalized geometry of AP, OIRS, and users
(single-step episodes, no bootstrapping, discount irrelevant). Action:
$\mathbf a=[\gamma,\omega]\in[-1,1]^2$ affinely mapped to
$[-\pi/2,\pi/2]^2$ (constraint 26a). Reward (mode \texttt{adr}): one fresh
draw of all $MN$ cosines per user,
$r=\frac{1}{K}\sum_k \mathrm{ADR}_k / r_{\mathrm{scale}}$ with
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{ADR}_k=\frac{B}{2}\log_2\!\left[1+\frac{e}{2\pi}
\left(\frac{P\,R_{\mathrm{PD}}\,Z_k}{Q}\right)^{2}\right].
\end{equation}
Reward (mode \texttt{aoi}): $r=-\max_k \overline{\Delta}_{k,\mathrm{IR}}$,
i.e.\ the paper's min--max AoI objective (Eq.~26), computed in closed form via
the Gamma CDF (the Meijer $G^{1,1}_{1,2}$ terms reduce to regularized
incomplete gamma functions).

\section{The Five DRL Models}

All agents share: two-hidden-layer MLPs of width 64, Adam optimization,
identical 6{,}000-interaction budget, identical seed, and 200 uniform
warm-up actions (where applicable).

\subsection{DDPG (\texttt{ddpg.py})}
Deterministic actor $\mu_\theta(s)$ with $\tanh$ output; critic
$Q_\phi(s,a)$. Because episodes are single-step, the critic target is the
immediate stochastic reward,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L(\phi)=\E\bigl[(Q_\phi(s,a)-r)^2\bigr],\qquad
\nabla_\theta J=\E\bigl[\nabla_a Q_\phi(s,a)\big|_{a=\mu_\theta(s)}
\nabla_\theta\mu_\theta(s)\bigr],
\end{equation}
so $Q_\phi$ learns the \emph{expected-ADR surface} over the orientation
randomness and the actor ascends it. Exploration: additive Gaussian noise
($\sigma=0.3$).

\subsection{SAC (\texttt{sac.py})}
Stochastic $\tanh$-Gaussian actor with the change-of-variables log-density
correction, twin soft critics with a minimum operator, and---the decisive
improvement over a hand-tuned variant---\emph{automatic temperature}:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L(\alpha)=-\alpha\,\E\bigl[\log\pi(a|s)+\bar{\mathcal H}\bigr],
\qquad \bar{\mathcal H}=-\dim(\mathcal A)=-2,
\end{equation}
initialized at $\alpha_0\approx0.1$. This lets the exploration bonus adapt to
the ADR reward scale; with a fixed, too-large $\alpha$ the entropy term
dominates the noisy reward and the policy never sharpens.

\subsection{PPO (\texttt{ppo.py})}
Clipped-surrogate objective with a learned value baseline,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L(\theta)=-\E\Bigl[\min\bigl(\rho_t A_t,\;
\mathrm{clip}(\rho_t,1\!-\!\epsilon,1\!+\!\epsilon)A_t\bigr)\Bigr],
\qquad \rho_t=\tfrac{\pi_\theta(a_t|s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{\mathrm{old}}}(a_t|s_t)},
\end{equation}
with $A_t=r_t-V(s_t)$ (single-step). Fixes applied for the noisy-reward
setting: on-policy batch of 256 draws so the advantage estimate averages the
orientation randomness, 15 epochs per batch with an approximate-KL early stop
(0.03), and a linearly decaying entropy bonus. PPO remains the least
sample-efficient of the five here, which is expected: each policy update
discards its data, whereas the off-policy agents reuse every draw.

\subsection{CAPG (\texttt{capg.py})}
Continuous-action likelihood-ratio policy gradient,
\begin{equation}
\nabla_\theta J=\E\bigl[\nabla_\theta\log\pi_\theta(a|s)\,(r-\bar b)\bigr],
\end{equation}
with an exponential-moving-average baseline $\bar b$ (variance reduction), a
decaying entropy bonus, and a floored log-std preventing premature policy
collapse while the reward is still noisy.

\subsection{ECDPO (\texttt{ecdpo.py}) --- \textcolor{red}{placeholder}}
The proposed algorithm's update equations were not available, so this file
implements a clearly-marked \emph{enhanced continuous deep policy optimizer}
on a DDPG backbone with four flag-controlled enhancements suited to the
stochastic ADR reward: (E1) twin critics with a minimum operator against
noise-driven overestimation; (E2) delayed actor updates; (E3) exponentially
decaying exploration noise $0.5\!\to\!0.05$ (global search first, fine
alignment later); (E4) Huber critic loss, robust to rare tail draws of the
truncated-Laplace orientation. \textbf{Replace this backbone with the actual
ECDPO update rule before reporting results under that name}; the flags make
ablation one-liners.

\subsection{Cross-layer fairness: the ADR/AoI trade-off}
At the mean-ADR optimum the narrow beam ($\kappa=20$) serves users 2--3 and
\emph{abandons user~1} ($\E[\mathrm{ADR}_1]=0$, worst-case
$\overline{\Delta}_{1,\mathrm{IR}}=4.5$)---precisely the unfairness that
motivates the paper's min--max AoI objective. Retraining ECDPO with the
\texttt{aoi} reward on the wide-beam fairness configuration ($\kappa=6$,
$\bar\gamma=3\times10^{15}$) yields $\gamma=2.49^\circ$,
$\omega=-10.33^\circ$ with per-user AoI $[1.5,\,1.5,\,1.5]$---the theoretical
minimum (every user decodes in HARQ round~1), matching the min--max grid
reference $\overline{\Delta}_{\max}=1.5$ exactly. The DRL framework therefore
supports both the throughput objective (P1) and the cross-layer AoI objective
(Eq.~26) by switching one reward flag.
